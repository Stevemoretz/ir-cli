On his return, Cesar married Pompeia, a granddaughter of Sulla. Their marriage ended in scandal.
In 63 BC, Cesar had been elected Pontifex Maximus, receiving as his official residence a house on the Via Sacra.
Here the sacred rites of the Bona Dea, from which all men were excluded, were celebrated in the winter of 62.
But an ambitious young nobleman named Publius Claudius Pulcher entered the house disguised as a woman, ostensibly for the purpose of seducing Pompeia.
