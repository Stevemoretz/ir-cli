According to the Roman historians, Cesar's murder was foretold by a number of ill omens, as well as the Etruscan haruspex Spurinna,
who warned him of great personal danger either on or by the Ides of March in 44 BC.
The night before his assassination, Calpurnia dreamed that Cesar had been wounded, and lay dying in her arms.
In the morning, she begged him not to meet the senate, as he had planned, and moved by her distress and entreaties, he resolved not to go.
